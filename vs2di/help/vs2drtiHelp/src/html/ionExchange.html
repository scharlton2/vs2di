Ion Exchange

Ion exchange describes a reaction of the form:

m(c1)n + n(cs2) = m(cs1) + n(c2)m

where
cs1 = concentration of ion 1 in solid phase
c1 = concentration in ion 1 fluid phase
cs2 = concentration of ion 2 in solid phase
c2 = concentration in ion 2 fluid phase
n = valence for ion 1
m = valence for ion 2

Four types of ion exchange reactions are defined as follows:

  • Monovalent-monovalent: m = n = 1
  • Monovalent-divalent: m = 2, n = 1
  • Divalent-Monovalent: m = 1, n = 2
  • Divalent-divalent: m = 2, n = 2
