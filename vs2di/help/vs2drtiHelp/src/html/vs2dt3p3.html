VS2DRT

VS2DRT is a computer program for solving problems of water flow and solute transport in variably saturated porous media.

VS2DRT is a standalone program that reads simulation setup data from input files, and writes simulation results to output files.

Major features of VS2DRT include the following:

• Use of finite-difference approximations to solve the Richard's equation for flow, and the advection-dispersion equation for heat and solute transport.
• Simulated regions can be one-dimensional columns, two-dimensional vertical cross sections, or three-dimensional axially symmetric cylinders.
• Boundary conditions for flow include: specified pressure head, specified flux, infiltration with ponding, plant transpiration, bare soil evaporation, seepage faces, and gravity drains.
• Unsaturated hydraulic characteristics may be represented by: van Genuchten, Brooks-Corey, Haverkamp, and Rossi-Nimmo equations, as well as tabular data.
• Transport capabilities include both heat and multicomponent solute transport and the reaction capabilities are a complete implementation of geochemical reactions of PHREEQC.
