Tutorial: Domain

To view and/or modify the model domain, select "Domain" from the "Active Data" Selector (see ).

The simulated domain is roughly rectangular in shape. The top of the domain represents land surface. There is a slight depression in the center of the surface, which represents an ephemeral stream. the stream is assumed to be flowing for 0.1 day with a water temperature of 30oC and a 0.1 molar concentration of sodium chloride (Solution 2 in the Phreeqc input file "tutorial.dat"). For the next 10 days solute-free water (Solution 1) with a temperature of 10oC infiltrates through the entire top boundary. A specified flux boundary condition is used. The bottom of the domain is assumed to be impermeable rock upon which a perched water table may exist; the temperature of that boundary is held constant at 12oC. The side boundaries are treated as no-flow boundaries except near the bottom where seepage faces are allowed to develop above the water table.
